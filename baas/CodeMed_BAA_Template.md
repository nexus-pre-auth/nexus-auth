### RECITALS

WHEREAS, Covered Entity is a "covered entity" as defined in the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996 ("HIPAA") and the regulations promulgated thereunder;

WHEREAS, Covered Entity desires to disclose certain protected health information to Business Associate for the purpose of claim processing and denial recovery services;

WHEREAS, Business Associate agrees to receive, maintain, and use such protected health information in compliance with HIPAA and this Agreement;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the mutual promises and covenants contained herein, the parties agree as follows:

---

### 1. DEFINITIONS

**1.1** Terms used but not defined in this Agreement shall have the same meaning as set forth in HIPAA and the HITECH Act.

**1.2** "Protected Health Information" or "PHI" shall have the same meaning as "protected health information" in 45 CFR §160.103, limited to the information created, received, maintained, or transmitted by Business Associate on behalf of Covered Entity.

**1.3** "Breach" shall have the same meaning as in 45 CFR §164.402.

**1.4** "HIPAA Rules" means the Privacy, Security, Breach Notification, and Enforcement Rules at 45 CFR Part 160 and Part 164.

---

### 2. OBLIGATIONS OF BUSINESS ASSOCIATE

**2.1 Permitted Uses and Disclosures.** Business Associate may use and disclose PHI only as necessary to perform the following services on behalf of Covered Entity (the "Services"):

- Claim processing and submission
- Denial recovery for CO-16, CO-50, and CO-97 codes
- Appeal letter generation
- Analytics and reporting (de-identified only)
- Any other uses required by law

**2.2 Prohibited Uses.** Business Associate shall not:

- Use PHI in any manner that violates HIPAA
- Sell PHI
- Use PHI for marketing purposes
- Disclose PHI to any third party except as expressly permitted

**2.3 Safeguards.** Business Associate shall:

- Implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards that reasonably and appropriately protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of PHI
- Encrypt all PHI at rest using AES-256 or equivalent
- Encrypt all PHI in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Maintain access logs of all PHI accessed
- Limit access to PHI to authorized personnel only

**2.4 Reporting of Breaches.** Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Breach of unsecured PHI within 72 hours of discovery. The report shall include:

- Description of the Breach
- Identification of each individual whose PHI was involved
- Steps taken to mitigate harm
- Steps taken to prevent future Breaches

**2.5 Subcontractors.** Business Associate shall ensure that any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI agree to the same restrictions and conditions as set forth in this Agreement.

**2.6 Access to PHI.** Business Associate shall make PHI available to Covered Entity as necessary to meet Covered Entity's obligations under 45 CFR §164.524 (right of access).

**2.7 Amendment of PHI.** Business Associate shall make PHI available for amendment and incorporate any amendments as required by 45 CFR §164.526.

**2.8 Accounting of Disclosures.** Business Associate shall document disclosures of PHI and provide an accounting to Covered Entity as required by 45 CFR §164.528.

---

### 3. OBLIGATIONS OF COVERED ENTITY

**3.1 Permissible Requests.** Covered Entity shall not request Business Associate to use or disclose PHI in any manner that would violate HIPAA if done by Covered Entity.

**3.2 Accurate Information.** Covered Entity represents that all PHI provided to Business Associate is accurate and complete to the best of its knowledge.

**3.3 Notice of Privacy Practices.** Covered Entity shall provide Business Associate with its Notice of Privacy Practices.

**3.4 Consent.** Covered Entity represents that it has obtained all necessary consents and authorizations from individuals for the disclosure of PHI to Business Associate.

---

### 4. TERM AND TERMINATION

**4.1 Term.** This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and continue until terminated by either party.

**4.2 Termination for Cause.** Either party may terminate this Agreement immediately upon:

- Material breach by the other party that remains uncured for 30 days after written notice
- A determination that the other party has violated HIPAA in a material way

**4.3 Obligations Upon Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement for any reason, Business Associate shall:

- Return or destroy all PHI received from Covered Entity
- Retain no copies of PHI
- Provide Covered Entity with written certification of destruction

**4.4 Survival.** Sections 2.4 (Breach Reporting), 2.8 (Accounting), 4.3 (Return of PHI), and 5 (Indemnification) shall survive termination.

---

**5.2** Covered Entity shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless Business Associate from and against any and all losses, damages, claims, expenses, and attorneys' fees arising from Covered Entity's breach of this Agreement or violation of HIPAA.
